    {{Questbox | id = 80682 | name = One Last Story | disambigcat = Ringing Deeps | displaycat = The Ringing Deeps | category = Ringing Deeps | level = {{Level|Ringing Deeps}} | start = [[Brennok]] | end = [[Haimaz]] | money = {{cost|11|70}} | experience = 5,450 | previous = [[A Sip of Cinder]] | next = [[On the Road]] }}

    ==Objectives== Attend the recitation. * Attend the Recitation * Meet [[Brennok]] by the bridge

    ==Description== Well, it's time. I'll be holding the recitation at the mine entrance. I'll see you there, though don't feel obligated to stay the whole time.

    I'll leave my book for [[Haimaz]] to find. She'll take care of it.

    After the ceremony, I would like to say goodbye properly. Meet me by the bridge outside of town. I'll head there after the recitation.

    ==Rewards== You will receive: {{cost|11|70}} 5,450 [[XP]]

    ==Completion== I knew Brennok was acting odd. Then one of the miners mentioned you'd borrowed a spare set of clothes. That and his strange mood, well, it wasn't hard to guess that he planned to leave.

    I expected him to come this way, though. Have you seen him?

    ==Notes== [[File:One Last Story.jpg|thumb|Brennok telling a story]] On accept: :{{text|say|Haimaz|Go on, you two. I have a few things to take care of. I'll catch up.}} :''Brennok leaves [[Brennok's Chronicle]] on one of the wheels of the cart that Haimaz is leaning on before walking off.''

    The chronicle reads: {{book|Brennok's Chronicle|content=

    Haimaz,

    There has long been a fault in me. A seam of doubt and longing. Every day, it threatens to crack me apart. How long am I to live only for others? What good are stories from one who has lived none? And how long will you be forced to humor me? I'm going. I'm not sure where. I don't think I'll be back. I'm sorry.

    Signed,
    Brennok}}

    Head up to the recitation site and speak with Brennok: {{Dialogue|class=plainlist| Well, this is it. The last story I'll tell for some time.

    I don't see Haimaz. She doesn't always attend. Titans know she's heard my stories plenty.

    Still, I'd hoped... but she wouldn't know this would be my last recitation.
