    '''Local Flavors''' concludes the Winterpelt Language skill 25 portion of the [[Winterpelt Furbolg]] storyline.

    ==Objectives== Ask [[Kazzi]] for ingredients to flavor the brew. * Brewing Supplies Collected {{co|65.8|12.7|Azure Span}}

    ==Description== Now that we have the base o' the brew figured out, we need tae add the spice.

    Put that rudimentary [[Winterpelt Furbolg|Winterpelt]] I taught ye tae use and ask around for local ingredients tae blend into the flavor profile. Try talkin' with Kazzi. From what I gather she's somethin' of a [[Vendor|trader]].

    The finished brew will be a mix of [[Dwarf|dwarven]] and Winterpelt traditions which seems appropriate for the circumstances.

    An' if we're lucky, it won't taste half bad!

    ==Rewards== You will receive: * [[Winterpelt Totem]] * {{cost|28|9}} * 9,500 [[XP]] * +3000 [[reputation]] with [[Winterpelt Furbolg]]

    ==Progress== Get out there an' ask. She won't bite.

    Unless ya inadvertently ask her tae, I suppose.

    ==Completion== Right, let's see what we can do with these.

    ==Notes== Speak with [[Kazzi]], the [[Winterpelt Furbolg]] quartermaster across the river to the west: :[[File:Kazzi gossip skill 25.jpg]] :You're not so bad, outsider. We can do business.

    :{{gossip|}} :[[File:Kazzi gossip skill 25 2.jpg]] : :So you are looking for strong flavors? :Well, frostslip stems certainly fit the bill. I never could stomach the taste myself, but a tea brewed with them is supposed to help with almost any disease. :Trailpack is a classic, of course. Don't let the name fool you, I eat the stuff all the time. How do you feel about macerated fish livers and berries? :Pickled bitterblue is a personal favorite. The raw stuff is inedible, but once you pickle it for a few months? Delicious.

    {{gossip|Give me the first one, I guess.}} {{gossip|Let's go with the second option.}} *{{gossip|The third one, definitely.}}

    Pick any option: :{{text|say|Kazzi|[Furbolg] Enjoy! I didn't catch what you were doing with it, but I hope it works out for you.}}

    The options do provide different items, however:

    [[Brewing Supplies?#Option 1]] Tough and fibrous, with a smell that might best be described as... fishy licorice?

    [[Brewing Supplies?#Option 2]] This seems to be a fine slurry of animal viscera and winter berries suspended in fat and frozen. It smells faintly of... minty sausage?

    [[Brewing Supplies?#Option 3]] These rubbery, bitter lichen have been preserved in a sweet brine, adding a tangy kick that cuts through the sour, earthen smell they exude.

    The default quest item is the following [[Brewing Supplies?]] The Winterpelt assure you that these are, indeed, edible. Despite the smell.

    On complete: :{{text|say|Sonova Snowden|Sweet... tangy... and with a funk that's surprisin' but not unwelcome.}} :{{text|say|Sonova Snowden|It's no Barleybrew Scalder, but it'll keep the chill off ye and tastes a sight better than most o' what gets served around here!}}

    This concludes the skill 25 portion of the storyline, and brings players to {{text|friendly|friendly}} standing with the [[Winterpelt Furbolg]]. The next step begins at skill 50 when [[Garz]], [[Jyrra "Fuzzy Feat" Coldsoar"]] and [[Saza]] have a conversation to start [[A Living History]].
